That was devastating wasn't it?

Climate hazards are naturally occurring throughout the world in weather cycles. Natural disasters are normal and has been occurring since the world began. Droughts, floods, bushfires, hurricanes all start naturally. However as of 2021 we are witnessing destruction beyond the natural means of the world.

In recent years especially after the industrial revolution, there have been a series of devastating weather events. According to the World Meteorological Organisation the number of disasters has increased by a factor of five in half a century, further driven by climate change more and more extreme weather events are occurring and being reported. Although natural disasters have been happening the severity of these disasters are exponentially increasing due to climate change.

How does climate change worsen natural disasters?

According to the United States Geological Survey (USGS), a government organisation that monitors, assesses, and conducts targeted science research, increasing global surface temperatures caused by climate change will also increase the possibility of more droughts and intensify storms. As more water vapour is evaporated into the atmosphere it becomes a fuel for more storms to develop. More heat in the atmosphere and warmer ocean surface temperatures can lead to increased wind speeds in tropical storms. This means that although the number of natural disasters remain constant, the intensity and destructiveness of those are increasing.

What is climate change?

Climate change is defined as a significant change of average weather conditions. This includes getting warmer, wetter, or drier weather over a number of decades. Although this does not impact us instantly, it is the longer term trend that is concerning. Our globe naturally goes through carbon absorbing phases long before humans were around. This is where the carbon dioxide levels rises and gets absorbed by natural means, which includes trees, plants and other natural greens to help regulate CO2 levels. However since the industrial revolution when coal burning got introduced for manufacturing, the world got introduced to carbon dioxide emissions that have never been seen before.

The main driver for climate change is the greenhouse effect.

As humans produce more and more greenhouse gases (methane, nitrous oxide and most prominently carbon dioxide) it creates an ozone layer that traps the natural heat produced by the sun. These heat waves are usually reflected back or absorbed naturally through photo synthesis and other natural processes of our plants and forests. However with the exponential continuous increase of greenhouse gases during the industrial revolution, the ozone layer has become so thick that heat from the sun is trapping heat within the atmosphere.

Humans are increasingly influencing the climate and Earth's temperature by our daily activities; burning fossil fuels, deforestation and farming livestock. Since the industrial revolution burning coal, oil and gas has been dramatically increased for production and manufacturing.

Burning of fossil fuel for energy

When fossil fuels are burned, they release large amounts of carbon dioxide, a greenhouse gas into the air. Greenhouse gases then continue to trap heat in our atmosphere through the greenhouse effect, causing global warming.

Transportation

According to Climate Council Australia, transport is Australia's third largest source of greenhouse gas emissions with the highest rate of growth totalling to 17% of Australia's total emissions. Collectively, cars in Australia emit the same amount of pollution as Queensland's entire fossil fuelled electricity supply.

Deforestation

Clearing of forests affects the local temperatures and precipitation patterns as it reduces rainfall and disrupts the natural water cycle. The act of deforestation alone emits large amounts of carbon dioxide into the air. However the most concerning consequence is trees, in which they take up atmospheric carbon dioxide and convert that into energy and oxygen.

Food and agriculture

In 2019, agriculture alone produced 13% of Australia's greenhouse gases. Activities such as deforestation for agricultural lands and emissions of greenhouse gases through the enteric fermentation of ruminant livestock has detrimental effects to global warming. Cows and other livestock contribute 37% of human activity related methane emissions (More potent greenhouse gas than carbon dioxide).

How is this happening?

Technology has made a great leap forward in tackling the fight against climate change. With the ever increasing price of energy companies, individuals and governments are desperately trying to find ways to reduce the cost and the toll it takes on the Earth. There has been many incredible inventions that have done considerable change in our fight against global warming.

One thing that was noted during the research was that individuals often take action when it does not disrupt their comfortable lives. Entrepreneurs are coming up with more innovative solutions to replace harmful daily emissions with cleaner greener alternatives.

Cleaner greener alternatives

Renewable energy

Renewable sources of energy are not being considered as too expensive in recent times. With the significant reduction in cost of lithium batteries and the materials needed to implement sources of energy from the sun (Solar Panels), flowing water, and wind. Many different homes, companies and countries are implementing various sources of energy and diversifying their energy portfolio. This takes a tremendous stress away from non-renewable sources of energy.

Electric Vehicles

With the reduction of in cost of lithium batteries many car manufacturers are adopting electric vehicles as the future. Leading the pack is Tesla, one of the world's largest auto mobile manufacturers building affordable, comfortable and technologically advanced electric vehicles to pave the way to eliminating gas cars. Cars are not the only vehicles that has seen leaps in improvements, no emissions buses, trains and trucks are also becoming more and more common.

Deforestation agreements

As of 2020, 473 companies have made commitments to eliminate deforestation from their supply chains. Companies and large corporations that deal with illegal mining operations are the main cause large scale deforestation. These commitments are the first step in large companies taking accountability for their actions and implement changes on a public promise. This promotes transparency in their operations and influential companies send a strong message to the world.

Alternative meats

With a global food shortage along with all the global emissions that comes from feeding 9 billion people, there needs to be an alternative solution. There are many different types of alternatives to meat which substitutes the meat with lab-grown meat. These substitutes are always improving to taste, feel and smell like the real thing. The most notable company is Beyond Meat who created the world's first meat burger based entirely plant based made from vegetable protein found in peas.

What is our future?

Building a renewable future.

Countries all around the world are changing to be more climate conscious. Places such as Europe, Australia, and the United States are all exploring and diversifying their energy portfolio, some slower than others. There is no doubt that alternative meats, cars and energy are our future and in these 2 decades alone major contributions have been made. Although countries still heavily rely on coal, gas and oil as energy sources, the implementation of diversifying energy consumption in countries are significantly noticeable.

Not just corporations. Not just countries.

Although large corporations and their sources of energy has made a significant change in lowering emissions, this positive future is largely attributed to individual efforts to be more deliberate in their sources of energy and consumption. Solar panels, electric vehicles and alternative meats are becoming mainstream which is one of the biggest changes in the past decade. Using renewable energy is a self conscious decision individuals can make to make an impact now. Collectively, as electric vehicles and solar panels become more affordable this fight against climate change seems more achievable. Entrepreneurs are still finding ways to re-imagine a green world and even ways to remove carbon emissions in the air.
