What did you just witness?

Climate hazards are naturally occurring throughout the world in weather cycles. Natural disasters are normal and has been occurring since the world began. Droughts, floods, bushfires, hurricanes all start naturally however as of 2021 we are witnessing destruction beyond the natural means of the world.

In recent years especially after the industrial revolution there have been a series of devastating weather events. According to the World Meteorological Organisation the number of disasters has increased by a factor of five in half a century, further driven by climate change more and more extreme weather events are occurring and being reported. Although natural disasters have been happening the severity of these disasters are exponentially increasing due to climate change.

How does climate change worsen natural disasters?

According to the United States Geological Survey (USGS), a government organisation that monitors, assess and conducts targeted science research. With the increasing global surface temperatures caused by climate change the possibility of more droughts and increased intensity of storms will likely occur. As more water vapour is evaporated into the atmosphere it becomes a fuel for more storms to develop. More heat in the atmosphere and warmer ocean surface temperatures can lead to increased wind speeds in tropical storms. This means that although the number of natural disasters remain constant the intensity and destructiveness of those are increasing.

WHAT IS CLIMATE CHANGE?

The term climate change kept appearing when describing these events but its effect on these devastating events but what is climate change?

We have highlighted what happens when climate change affects our globe too widely but how did this happen? Our globe naturally goes through carbon absorbing phases where the carbon dioxide levels rises and gets absorbed by natural means, this can include trees, plants and other natural greens to help regulate CO2 levels. However since the industrial revolution when coal burning got introduced for manufacturing the world got introduced to carbon dioxide emissions like never seen before.

Humans are increasingly influencing the climate and Earth's temperature by our daily activities; burning fossil fuels, deforestation and farming livestock.

The main driver for climate change is the greenhouse effect.
